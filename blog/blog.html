First Week of Coding: What I Learned at Correlation One
(posted: May 5, 2023)

Correlation One is an education company offering online bootcamps dedicated to preparing students for a technologically-based workforce by building a network of "fellows" proficient in areas such as data analytics, cybersecurity, cloud support and software development.

Through a partnership with Amazon's Career Choice program, which offers paid education or re-skilling to all their Level 1 through Level 4 associates who have worked for the company for 90 days, Correlation One (C1) commenced training the third cohort of their five-month Software Development Training Program in early July.

Comprised of a nearly 250 Amazon associates from all over the United States, the first class was held virtually via Zoom on April 5, 2023. C1's robust structure of skilled instructors, TAs, TA groups (of students) and professional development counselors ensures no one will be left behind. Students, enthused and enthusiastic, appear eager to help and encourage each other.

The first week of classes was intense but involved little actual coding instruction. Instead, instructors gave presentations that provided an overview of expectations and course structure. We saw videos recorded by successful course graduates extolling the virtues of the program. No one pretended it would be easy, but everyone emphasized that all fellows would receive any help they needed if they met instructors halfway. The fact that scheduling is geared to fit around Amazon's unique scheduling challenges is a distinct advantage.

A considerable benefit of the course is the Slack workspace set up for the students. It serves as an online classroom, commons, community center and study hall. Various channels, each with a different mission, allow fellows to drop in at any time and interact at will, whether to post a meme or get assistance with a difficult coding problem. A live chat during thrice weekly two-hour live seminars allows any student to ask questions in real time, secure in the knowledge that a TA or other students will pitch in to assist and answer their question.

There was no actual coding instruction during our first few days of lessons but the promise of learning to code, a dream and aspiration of the fellows for many reason -- higher pay, more freedom, the possibility of remote work -- was finally within reach. The next few months promise to be challenging but satisfying. If the actual lessons are as inspiring and engaging as our first week "preview" the challenge will be worth it.
